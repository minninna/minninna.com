This space sits next to the photographs.

If the rest of the site is made of images taken with a camera, this is where the images without a camera live.

Because photography, at least to me, does not begin with a device. It begins with observation. With the quiet act of noticing. You can photograph with a lens, or you can photograph without one, by paying attention, by staying with what is in front of you long enough for it to reveal something.

My Musings is that second space. A private area.

A kind of notebook. Open, unfinished by design.

Some entries might be fragments. Others might remain drafts. Observations caught in passing. Small anecdotes, almost like those curious pieces you find in old magazines. Thoughts on how things shift, culturally, socially, individually. Reflections that move between the personal and the collective, between what we are becoming and what we might be leaving behind.

Nothing here is meant to be definitive.

This is a place for notes, not statements. For questions more than answers. For thinking in public without the need to conclude.

A free space.

It does not feel accidental that it begins today, on a day that here carries the meaning of freedom. Not only as a historical moment, but as a principle. A reminder.

So this will be that. A small exercise in freedom.

To observe.
To write.
To leave things open.
